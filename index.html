GPPC2

The Competition

Computing paths for moving agents is a core challenge in many application areas, such as robotics and computer games. Grid-based models offer a simple and effective way to tackle this problem, with many new works appearing throughout the academic literature. Research in this area is most often motivated by practical considerations, and the resulting algorithmic techniques usually come with tradeoffs, such as solution quality vs. runtime performance vs. compute resource costs (CPU, memory etc.). The Grid-Based Path Planning Competition (GPPC2) is a forum for tracking and disseminating state-of-the-art progress in the area. It is the fourth edition of the competition, with previous competitions in 2012, 2013 and 2014.

The goals of the competition are:

  • To identify important topics in Grid-based Path Planning and to establish clear baselines for state-of-the-art performance.
  • To promote best practice for experimental evaluation, including suitable metrics, analysis methods and benchmarking techniques.
  • To disseminate results to the broader community and track progress over time.
  • To promote and grow research in the area of Grid-based Path Planning.

We invite submissions from established researchers, active practitioners and interested students. Participation in the competition can help to grow confidence in the practical suitability of existing (in-production) methods or in newly proposed algorithmic techniques.

Competition Setup

GPPC2 currently measures progress in three distinct tracks:

  • Classical track, which is 8-connected pathfinding on a static grid map.
  • Any-angle track, where grid paths are not restricted to eight directions.
  • Dynamic track, which introduces periodically changing grid maps for 8-connected pathfinding.

All tracks run continuously and submissions are welcome at any time. Participants can submit multiple times and their submission will be evaluated automatically. Results are available in real-time and progress in the competition is indicated using a live leaderboard.

On an annual basis a snapshot of results will be taken and used to create a report. This report will summarise the advances made through the year and the current state of the art. A summary of these results will be presented at the next edition of the International Symposium on Combinatorial Search (SoCS).

How to Participate

  • Login with your Github account.
  • Download the start kit for the classic, anyangle, or dynamic track.
  • Read the submission instructions and integrate your solving technique.
  • Submit your first entry!
  • Track your progress via the leaderboard!

Evaluation

All GPPC2 submissions are evaluated using a standard benchmark problem set. These problems comprise grid maps and pathfinding instances that are drawn from real applications and/or which are synthetically generated.

The evaluation process is threefold: (i) (optional) offline preprocessing, where auxiliary data can be generated; (ii) offline validation, which checks that every test problem can be successfully solved and; (iii) online evaluation, where algorithmic performance is measured according to several distinct metrics (see Q&A for the details). A submission is considered successful if it passes validation and solves every problem during evaluation.

Ranking and Results

In GPPC2 there is no single winner but a pareto-frontier of undominated entries. This approach reflects the many distinct settings where Grid-based Path Planning is applied, and where performance is often regarded from different perspectives.

Authors of successful submissions, which are undominated for any period during a given year, will be invited to co-author the annual report. The report will appear in the archival proceedings of the International Symposium on Combinatorial Search (SoCS).

Important Dates

Submission Deadline: None! Submit anytime!

Annual Report Deadline: to be eligible for inclusion in the next annual report, undominated submissions must be received by 1st May.
